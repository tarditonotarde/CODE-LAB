SynapHelmet is a futuristic immersive health app inspired by the 'Black Museum' episode of Black Mirror, Season 4. Users act as virtual doctors experiencing patients’ symptoms through an 'empathy helmet' brain-connection technology, gaining insights through VR-style simulations, interactive panels, and holographic interfaces.

Goals

  • Provide immersive empathy experiences for medical staff.
  • Visualize patient symptoms with real-time, interactive holographic displays.
  • Enable ethical evaluation of patient condition through virtual simulations.
  • Create emotionally-driven, futuristic interfaces with intuitive interactions.

Problem Statement

Traditional health training lacks immersive empathy tools. SynapHelmet allows users to safely experience patient symptoms in a controlled, VR-style environment, addressing both clinical training and emotional awareness challenges.

Research

  • Target Users & Needs
    • Medical staff, trainees, healthcare professionals
    • Real-time symptom visualization and metrics
    • VR/Immersive experience for empathy training
    • Holographic feedback, HUD panels, and interactive controls
    • Ethical evaluation, safety, and privacy considerations

Design Process

  • Storyboard Screens
    • Patient Selection: Horizontal 3D floating cards, hover tilt, Electric Blue active glow
    • Symptom Immersion: VR-style environment, abstract color-coded symptoms with particle effects
    • Observation & Notes Panel: Floating panel with gesture inputs and pulsing severity sliders
    • Suggested Actions: Holographic cards, animated arrows, particle sparks for urgency
    • Ethics & Safety: Central warning panel with pulsing neon icons and fading alerts
  • Interactions & Navigation
    • Swipe, drag, tap, gesture-based navigation
    • Smooth holographic dissolve transitions (0.3–0.5s)
    • Shadow cues and glitch effects during extreme symptoms
    • Floating particles and light reflections for depth

SynapHelmet provides medical staff with safe, immersive empathy experiences, enhancing emotional awareness, diagnostic insights, and clinical decision-making through VR-based symptom simulations, holographic interfaces, and interactive storytelling.
