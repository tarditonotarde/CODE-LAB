Trashtour Magazine offers a unique perspective on urban exploration, allowing readers to discover cities without physically moving. Each biannual issue focuses on a different city, using a single metro line as the structural backbone to unveil cultural narratives, contemporary art, urban landscapes, and human stories.

Concept & Approach

Urban Journey: Trashtour takes its readers from the outskirts to the heart of the city. Each metro station along the route becomes a chapter, sometimes absurd, sometimes amusing, sometimes deeply serious.

Immersive Reading: The format mirrors the pulse of urban life, offering a dynamic and immersive reading experience.

Editorial Structure

  • One City, One Metro Line: Each issue is dedicated to a single city, using a metro line as the thematic and structural guide.
  • Diverse & Multidimensional Content: From contemporary art features to kitsch photo essays capturing subcultures.
  • Urban Rhythms & Human Cartography: Mapping both geography and human landscapes, reflecting customs and local life.

Visual & Editorial Identity

Trashtour’s identity thrives on contrasts and eclectic urban storytelling:

  • Art & Culture: Features on exhibitions, creative movements, and contemporary art.
  • Neighborhood Stories: Narratives and histories from diverse districts.
  • Photographic Essays: Visual storytelling capturing absurd, humorous, or poignant city life moments.
  • Punk Aesthetics: Inspired by underground culture and metro chaos, blending zine spirit, graffiti, and raw visual energy.

The Experience

Readers embark on a literary and visual tour that mimics real metro journeys. At each stop, they are exposed to new perspectives, cultures, and stories. Trashtour is not just a magazine—it’s an urban adventure.

Conclusion

Trashtour Mag redefines travel storytelling by transforming metro lines into cultural arteries, proving that exploration doesn’t always require movement—sometimes, it only takes turning a page.
